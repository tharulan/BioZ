INTRODUCTION

As digital education rapidly advances, the need for emotionally adaptive and personalized learning solutions has become increasingly critical. This research introduces BioZ, a web-based self-learning platform developed specifically for Advanced Level Biology stream learners in Sri Lanka. BioZ integrates four key components designed to enhance academic engagement: (1) Stress detection through facial expressions and stress relief recommendations along with an AI-powered emotional health therapist; (2) a Knowledge sharing platform for social interactions with content-based categorization and suggestions; (3) an AI-driven question and answer generation system with integrated self-assessments; and (4) a Learner performance prediction and analytics system with performance related notifications. These modules collectively aim to address critical challenges in digital self-learning by fostering a domain centric and emotionally supportive educational environment. By enabling learners to monitor emotional states, engage in collaborative learning, receive automated formative assessments, and track academic progress, BioZ represents a comprehensive platform for promoting effective and personalized self-directed learning. The study contributes to the field of affective computing and intelligent learning systems, particularly within resource-constrained educational contexts.

IMPORTANCE OF SELF-LEARNING

Self-learning is a way of teaching yourself new skills without the aid of an instructor. If done correctly it can produce the best results since you know yourself the best, and you know what suits you. It's a great way to get ahead and gain knowledge without having to leave the comfort of your own index. Plus, you can take things at your own pace - no need to keep up with anyone else.
