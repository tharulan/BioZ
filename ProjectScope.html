Our PROJECT SCOPE

Literature Survey & Research Gap

Recent research highlights stress detection as a crucial factor in enhancing student engagement and emotional resilience. Traditional methods depended on expert intervention, but newer approaches leverage automated techniques. Sensor-based systems using physiological signals like EDA, heart rate, and temperature have dominated early work. More recently, non-invasive alternatives like facial expression recognition via CNNs are gaining traction for real-time stress detection. Several works integrate advanced deep learning models (e.g., VGG-16, ResNet-50) to optimize recognition. In parallel, recommendation systems based on deep reinforcement learning and ontology-driven frameworks are being explored to provide personalized stress relief and learning support. Educational personalization has been boosted by NLP and semantic technologies. AI models (T5, GPT-3) are being applied for content generation, student evaluation, and feedback. Collaborative learning platforms now incorporate context-aware recommendation using semantic graphs and RDF-based systems. Still, interpretability and scalability are recurring concerns, especially with black-box models. .

Research Problem

In the evolving landscape of digital education, self-directed learners often face significant challenges related to emotional regulation, personalized support, and the ability to process complex academic content independently. Existing e-learning systems typically focus on content delivery, overlooking the emotional and cognitive needs that are critical for sustained engagement and academic success. There is a clear lack of holistic, integrated solutions that simultaneously address stress detection, intelligent content recommendation, comprehension support, and performance monitoring. This research aims to bridge that gap by developing a multi-component framework that combines real-time stress detection through facial expression analysis, ontology-based emotional support, AI-driven knowledge sharing, automatic question generation and evaluation, and predictive performance analytics. By aligning emotional well-being with academic progress, the proposed system seeks to enhance the self-directed learning experience through adaptive, personalized, and psychologically aware educational support.

Research Objectives

The primary objective of this research is to develop an integrated, intelligent learning framework that enhances self-directed education through emotional awareness, personalized support, and performance monitoring. Specifically, the study aims to design a real-time stress detection system using facial expression analysis and machine learning techniques to classify emotional states during learning. Based on the identified stress levels, the system will provide personalized, ontology-based recommendations for stress relief and incorporate an AI-powered virtual therapist to offer psychological support and motivational dialogue. Additionally, the research seeks to build a semantic knowledge-sharing platform that supports collaborative learning through content-based categorization, intelligent recommendations, and ontology-driven semantic search. To improve content comprehension, the system will feature an automatic question generation and evaluation module that processes uploaded educational materials and provides instant feedback. Furthermore, the study will develop a performance prediction model using time-series analysis to forecast academic outcomes, identify at-risk learners, and generate personalized study plans aligned with learners' goals.


Methodology

<>The methodology of this research involves the design and implementation of a domain-specific, self-directed digital learning platform composed of four interconnected components, each targeting a specific aspect of learner support. First, a stress detection module leverages computer vision and a CNN model trained on facial expressions to classify real-time stress levels, which then trigger ontology-based coping recommendations and interaction with an NLP-driven virtual therapist for emotional support. Second, a knowledge-sharing platform enhances collaborative learning by enabling structured discussions, content-based categorization, and semantic search through ontology-backed NLP and visualization tools. Third, an AI-powered question and answer system automatically generates contextually relevant questions from uploaded biology content using transformer models, and evaluates student responses with GPT-3.5 to deliver instant feedback and scores. Finally, a performance prediction and analytics system applies ARIMA-based time series forecasting to quiz data, providing early identification of at-risk learners and generating personalized study plans via GPT-3.5 Turbo. The entire methodology emphasizes the integration of AI, ontologies, and personalized feedback mechanisms to foster emotional well-being and academic progress in self-directed learning.
